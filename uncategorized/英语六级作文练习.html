1.Employers should give their stuff at a 4-week holiday a year to make employees better at their jobs. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

Some people hold that employers should provide them with four weeks holidays a year to perform better in jobs. Personally, I fully agree this idea, since taking breaks from works can enhance employees' creativity and overall well-being.

Firstly, employees can get the chance to learn new skills and pursue personally hobbies by offering enough time. As a result, they more likely to come up with new idea, innovative solution and fresh perspectives to help company grow and succeed. Admittedly, employees can focus on health and spend more time with their family and friends by accompanying them to do leisure activities like taking a trip countryside. This can help employees to reduce stress, anxiety and burnout, which are prevalent in today's fast-paced work environment. When employee feel happier, they are more likely to be motivated and engaged in their work,leading to better job satisfaction and retention rates.

In conclusion, I strongly believe that employers should give their stuff at least four weeks holidays a year to improve their job performance.

2.Social media

It is very common to see people, browsing social media platforms, such as TikTok and Facebook, to contact with others and get them well-informed. Personally, I deem this commonplace advantageous.

On the one hand, social media provide its users with easier access to both their loved ones and strangers by overcoming geographic distance. It is possible for user to chat with each other in a virtual world just like the way they communicate in reality via social media using a mobile device. On the other hand, social media has an edge over traditional sources for like newspaper. To be specific, the news shown on social media may be mislead people, the stories reported on newspaper are rather authentic, since the strict censorship requires editors to be certain about minute details. However, this can in effect be prevented as long as readers foster the ability to seek credible sources and distinguish the information they have acquired.

In a nutshell, the downsides of social media pale into insignificance when set against its upsides.
